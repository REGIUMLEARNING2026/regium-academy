Digital Fingerprinting

Live Scan — State-by-State Guide

Live Scan captures fingerprints electronically and transmits them directly to state and FBI repositories. Here's what you need to know to offer this service in your state.

🖥️

Step 1: Choose Your Hardware

FBI-certified scanners include the CrossMatch GUARDIAN, Integrated Biometrics Watson Mini, and Suprema RealScan-G10. Prices range from $2,000–$8,000. Mobile USB options are available for field work.

📋

Step 2: Software & Certification

You need FBI-approved EBTS/F (Electronic Biometric Transmission Specification) compliant software. Many vendors bundle software with hardware. Your system must pass state ORI (Originating Agency Identifier) approval.

🏛️

Step 3: Apply for an ORI Number

Contact your state's Department of Justice or Criminal Justice Services to apply for an Originating Agency Identifier (ORI). This allows you to submit prints directly to the FBI CJIS Division.

🔗

Step 4: Connect to a Channeler

If you're not seeking your own ORI, partner with an FBI-approved channeler like Identogo, Idemia, or your state's authorized provider to route prints on your behalf.

📝

Step 5: State Authorization

Most states require you to register as an authorized Live Scan operator. This involves a background check, training certification, and annual renewal fees ranging from $50–$500.

💰

Step 6: Fee Structure

Live Scan fees include a rolling fee ($10–$25), state processing fee ($17–$75), and FBI processing fee ($13.25 for civil, $13.25 for criminal). Typical client charge: $50–$120 per session.

Know Before You Start

Issues That May Prevent You from Becoming a Fingerprint Technician

Being aware of these barriers upfront will save you time and money during the licensing process.

🔴 Criminal Record Disqualifiers

Felony convictions — especially for fraud, theft, or crimes against persons — will disqualify you in most states. Some states extend this to certain misdemeanors within the last 5–10 years. Always check your state's "look-back period."

🔴 Federal Disqualifiers (FBI CJIS)

Anyone seeking to access FBI CJIS systems (required for Live Scan operators) is subject to a federal background check. Disqualifiers include: terrorism, murder, sex offenses, and drug trafficking convictions — no waiver available.

🟡 Lack of Business License / Insurance

Operating without a valid business license or general liability insurance (typically $1M minimum) can result in fines or shutdown. Some states additionally require a surety bond of $10,000–$25,000.

🟡 Physical Conditions Affecting Fingerprint Quality

Eczema, psoriasis, manual labor-related wear, and certain skin conditions can temporarily prevent an individual from being fingerprinted. As the technician, you must know how to document and handle "no-print" situations.

🟡 Unauthorized Practice

In states that require licensure (e.g., California, Texas, Florida), operating without proper authorization is a misdemeanor or felony. Always secure required state registrations before offering services.

🟣 Age Requirements

Most states require applicants to be at least 18 years old to operate a fingerprinting service or access law enforcement systems. Some states set the minimum at 21 for certain licensing categories.

🟣 Equipment Failure / Non-Certified Equipment

Using non-FBI-certified equipment for Live Scan submissions will result in rejected prints. For ink fingerprinting, using expired or low-quality ink pads leads to FBI rejection — always use fresh, FBI-grade supplies.

🟣 Privacy Law Compliance

You must comply with state privacy laws governing biometric data storage and handling. States like Illinois (BIPA), Texas, and Washington have strict biometric privacy laws with substantial penalties for violations.

🟡 Failure to Maintain Certifications

Some states require ongoing training and annual renewals. Letting your certification lapse while continuing to operate creates significant legal exposure. Set calendar reminders for all renewal dates.

Entrepreneur Toolkit

Start Your Fingerprinting Business

Everything you need to launch, operate, and grow a profitable fingerprinting business from day one.

🏢

Form Your Business Entity

Register an LLC for liability protection. File with your Secretary of State ($50–$500). Get an EIN from the IRS (free). Open a dedicated business checking account. Estimated startup: $200–$600.

🛠️

Starter Equipment List

FD-258 fingerprint cards (100-pack ~$30), Identix ink pads (~$15), fingerprint rollers, magnifying loupe, disposable gloves, cleaning wipes, and a portable table for mobile services. Total ink setup: ~$200–$400.

🔐

FBI Channeler Partnerships

Partner with an FBI-approved channeler to process prints without obtaining your own ORI. Top channelers: Identogo (IDEMIA), Accurate Biometrics, and FieldPrint. Each state has designated approved providers.

💲

Pricing Your Services

Standard ink fingerprinting: $25–$75/person. Live Scan (if applicable): $50–$120. Mobile/on-site corporate service: $300–$800/session. Notary + fingerprint bundled services command premium pricing.

📣

Marketing Channels That Work

Google Business Profile is your #1 tool. Target schools, hospitals, staffing agencies, legal offices, and foster care agencies. Partner with HR departments for recurring contracts. Business cards in courthouses and DMV offices convert well.

📱

Canvassing Strategy

Door-to-door at local businesses, nonprofits, and churches that do background checks. Attend job fairs and community events. Offer free demonstrations to HR managers. Build a referral bonus program for existing clients.

🌐

Online Presence Setup

Claim your Google Business Profile immediately. Build a simple website ($0–$200/yr). List on Thumbtack, Angi, and local directories. Use "fingerprinting near me" as your primary SEO keyword.

📃

Government Vendor Registration

Register on SAM.gov for federal contracts. Apply to your state's small business vendor registry. Many school districts and municipalities require vendors in their approved database — get on these lists early.

🤝

Strategic Partnerships

Partner with notaries, process servers, HR consulting firms, and immigrant legal services. Offer co-branded packages. These partners become passive referral engines with minimal ongoing effort.
