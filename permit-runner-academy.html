Who Needs Permit Running Services?

Permit runners save clients hours — sometimes weeks — of standing in lines and navigating bureaucracy. The demand is constant and growing.

🏗️

General Contractors

Builders and GCs need permits pulled constantly — new construction, additions, renovations, and electrical/plumbing work across every project.

🏠

Real Estate Developers

Developers juggle dozens of projects simultaneously and outsource permit running to stay on schedule and avoid costly delays.

⚡

Electricians & Plumbers

Licensed trades pull permits for virtually every job. A reliable permit runner is invaluable to a busy subcontractor who can't spend half a day at the permit office.

🏢

Commercial Businesses

Restaurants, retail stores, and offices need signage permits, fire inspection permits, health department permits, and occupancy permits.

🚗

Auto Dealers & DMV Work

In many states, permit runners handle title transfers, dealer plates, and DMV filings on behalf of car dealerships.

🏛️

Law Firms & Title Companies

Attorneys and title companies use permit runners to pull lien searches, zoning certificates, and certificate of occupancy records.

🎪

Event Organizers

Special event permits, temporary food permits, noise waivers, and street closure permits require expediting for time-sensitive events.

🌿

Landscapers & Solar Installers

Landscaping, irrigation, and solar installation often require zoning, HOA, and municipal permits that clients don't want to handle themselves.

Sample Template

Permit Running Business Plan

Use this template as your starting point. Customize each section with your local market, rates, and target clients.

📋 Plan Sections

1. Executive Summary 2. Business Description 3. Market Analysis 4. Services & Pricing 5. Marketing Strategy 6. Operations 7. Financial Projections 8. Goals & Milestones

1. Executive Summary

[Your Business Name] is a professional permit expediting service based in [City, State]. We specialize in obtaining building, zoning, health, fire, and specialty permits on behalf of contractors, developers, and businesses throughout [Service Area]. Our goal is to reduce permit turnaround time and administrative burden for clients so they can focus on their core work.

💡 Keep this to 1–2 paragraphs. It should summarize your whole plan — write it last.

2. Business Description

Legal Structure: [LLC / Sole Proprietor / S-Corp]
Owner(s): [Your Name]
Founded: [Year]
Location: [Home-based / Office Address]
Service Area: [Counties / Cities / Statewide]

We provide permit running services to clients who cannot afford the time or resources to navigate local permitting offices. We act as the client's authorized agent to submit applications, track status, respond to reviewer comments, and obtain approved permits in the shortest possible time.

3. Market Analysis

Target Clients:

  • General contractors and homebuilders (primary market)
  • Specialty contractors — electricians, plumbers, HVAC, solar installers
  • Real estate developers and property management companies
  • Retail businesses, restaurants, and commercial tenants
  • Law firms and title companies requiring lien/zoning searches
📊 Research your local market: How many active contractors are in your county? Check your state's contractor license database for a count — that's your addressable market.

Competition: In most markets there are few dedicated permit runners. Most competition comes from general office assistants or contractors doing it themselves. A professional, reliable service can capture significant market share quickly.

4. Services & Pricing

  • Standard Permit Running: $75–$150 per permit (simple residential)
  • Commercial Permit Running: $150–$400 per permit (plans review required)
  • Rush/Expedited Service: Standard fee + 50–100% rush surcharge
  • Monthly Retainer: $500–$2,000/month for high-volume contractors
  • Lien/Zoning Research: $50–$125 per search
  • DMV/Title Running (if applicable): $35–$85 per transaction
💰 Tip: Retainer clients are your best revenue — even 3–4 retainer clients at $700/month = $2,100–$2,800 recurring monthly income before any per-permit work.

5. Marketing Strategy

  • Google Business Profile — optimized for "permit runner near me" and "permit expediter [city]"
  • Canvass active construction sites — introduce yourself to the GC or site super
  • Visit building supply stores (Home Depot Pro desk, lumber yards) and leave cards
  • Partner with contractor licensing schools and trade associations
  • Reach out to local real estate investor groups and developer associations
  • LinkedIn outreach to construction project managers and developers
  • Referral incentive: $25 credit per referred client who places an order

6. Operations

Equipment Needed:

  • Reliable vehicle (mileage is tax-deductible)
  • Laptop or tablet for online portal submissions
  • Scanner/printer for document preparation
  • Filing system (physical and digital) for client records
  • Business phone line or Google Voice number

Workflow: Client places order → You collect required documents → Submit application (in-person or online) → Track status → Respond to reviewer comments → Deliver approved permit to client → Invoice and collect payment.

⚙️ Startup cost estimate: $300–$800 total for business registration, insurance, scanner, and basic supplies.

7. Financial Projections (Year 1)

  • Month 1–2: Setup phase. 5–10 permits/month. Revenue: ~$500–$1,200
  • Month 3–4: Building clientele. 15–25 permits/month. Revenue: ~$1,500–$3,000
  • Month 5–6: First retainer client. Revenue: ~$3,000–$5,000/month
  • Month 7–12: 2–4 retainer clients + per-permit work. Revenue: ~$4,000–$8,000/month

Annual Year 1 Goal: $30,000–$60,000 gross revenue
Expenses: ~$5,000–$10,000 (insurance, mileage, marketing, tools)
Net Profit: ~$20,000–$50,000 Year 1

8. Goals & Milestones

  • ✅ Day 1–30: Business registered, insurance obtained, building department accounts opened
  • ✅ Day 30–60: First 10 paying clients, Google Business Profile live

Professional Standards

Do's & Don'ts for Permit Runners

These rules separate the professionals from those who burn bridges and lose clients.

✅ Do's
✓
Always confirm the exact documents required before visiting the permit office — requirements vary by municipality and permit type.
✓
Build relationships with building department staff. Being respectful and professional gets your submissions prioritized.
✓
Communicate proactively with clients — update them on status changes without them having to ask.
✓
Keep copies of every document you submit, stamped approvals, and all correspondence. Paper trails protect you.
✓
Use written service agreements for every client — even repeat customers. Define scope, fees, and timelines clearly.
✓
Verify that your client's contractor license is active before submitting permits requiring one — rejections waste everyone's time.
✓
Learn the online submission portals in your area. Jurisdictions increasingly prefer digital — get ahead of this curve.
✓
Carry general liability insurance. It protects you if a permit error causes a project delay and the client seeks damages.
❌ Don'ts
✗
Never guarantee approval timelines you can't control. Permit offices set their own review schedules — underpromise and overdeliver.
✗
Don't attempt to pull permits that legally require the contractor's license in restricted states — this exposes both you and your client.
✗
Never submit incomplete applications. A missing document wastes days and damages your reputation with building department staff.
✗
Don't ignore plan review comments. Respond quickly with clear corrections — delays compound fast.
✗
Never be combative with permit office staff. They have discretion in how fast they process your submissions.
✗
Don't take on more work than you can handle. A missed deadline on a commercial project can cause thousands in client losses.
✗
Never operate without a written client agreement. Verbal deals end in disputes over fees, scope, and responsibility.
✗
Don't neglect record keeping. The IRS, a client dispute, or an audit can come at any time — clean records protect your business.
Know Before You Start

Common Barriers for Permit Runners

Being aware of these issues upfront will save you time, money, and legal trouble.

🔴 Contractor License Restrictions

In states like California, Florida, and New York, certain permit types can only be pulled by the licensed contractor of record — not a third-party runner. Understand the rules in your state before marketing those services.

🔴 Unauthorized Practice of Law

If a permit issue escalates to a variance hearing or zoning board appeal, that may cross into legal practice. Refer those situations to a land use attorney — never represent a client in legal proceedings without a license.

🟡 Jurisdiction Fragmentation

In most states, permits are issued at the city or county level — not the state level. Requirements, portals, and processing times vary dramatically even within the same county. Plan to invest time learning each jurisdiction you serve.

🟡 Online Portal Lockouts

Many municipalities now require contractor-registered portal accounts to submit permits electronically. If your client doesn't share access, you may have to submit in person. Always clarify portal access with your client upfront.

🟡 Slow Municipal Review Times

Plan review timelines range from 1 business day (small towns) to 8–12 weeks (major cities like Miami or LA). Set client expectations based on your specific municipality — never promise timelines you can't control.

🟣 Insurance Requirements

Some municipalities and clients require proof of general liability insurance (typically $1M minimum) before accepting you as an authorized agent. Budget $400–$800/year for a basic GL policy for your permit service business.

🟣 Scope Creep

Clients may ask you to interpret plans, advise on building codes, or recommend contractors — tasks outside your role. Stick to your lane: submit, track, deliver. Refer technical questions to licensed professionals.

🟣 Permit Expirations

Most permits expire if work doesn't begin within 180 days, or if inspections aren't passed on schedule. Track expiration dates for your clients and notify them proactively — an expired permit means starting the process over.

Client Acquisition

Marketing Your Permit Running Business

The best permit runners build thriving businesses through relationships, visibility, and consistency — not advertising spend.

📍

Google Business Profile

Your #1 free tool. Set up and fully optimize your profile with "permit runner," "permit expediter," and "permit service" in your description. Post weekly updates. Collect Google reviews from every client.

🏗️

Canvass Active Job Sites

Drive around your area and identify active construction. Introduce yourself to the site superintendent or GC. Hand them a card and a simple one-page rate sheet. Follow up within 1 week.

🏪

Building Supply Stores

Ask to leave business cards at the contractor pro desk at Home Depot, Lowe's, and local lumber yards. These are daily stops for your target clients. Bring donuts — it works.

🤝

Contractor License Schools

Partner with local contractor licensing prep schools to hand out cards to new licensees. New contractors don't yet know how to navigate permit offices — you're solving an immediate problem for them.

📊

Real Estate Investor Groups

Attend local REIA (Real Estate Investor Association) meetings. Investors doing fix-and-flip or new construction are high-volume, repeat permit clients. One good contact here can mean dozens of orders.

💼

LinkedIn Outreach

Search "general contractor [your city]" and "project manager construction [your city]" on LinkedIn. Send short, direct connection messages. Offer a free first permit run to earn the relationship.

📧

Email Follow-Up Sequence

After every first meeting or card handout, send a 3-email sequence over 2 weeks: (1) Nice to meet you recap, (2) Your rate sheet + testimonial, (3) Final follow-up with a specific call to action. Most clients book after email 2 or 3.

💰

Referral Program

Offer existing clients $25 account credit or a free permit run for every new paying client they refer. Word-of-mouth referrals from happy contractors are worth more than any ad spend.

📋

Retainer Proposals

After completing 3–5 orders for a client, pitch a monthly retainer. "For $700/month I'll handle all your permit running with priority turnaround." This converts volume clients into predictable recurring revenue.
